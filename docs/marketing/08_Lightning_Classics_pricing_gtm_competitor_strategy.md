# Lightning Classics — pricing, go-to-market, competitor analysis and revenue strategy

Prepared: 28 April 2026  
Project status: live, moving into scaling  
Commercial direction: classic-literature portal plus self-published editions containing royalty-free text, background, short “lightning” version and implications for today; Kindle, EPUB, paperback and hardback

---

## 1. Commercial thesis

Lightning Classics should not compete with free public-domain text. It should compete with **confusion, intimidation and lack of context**.

The product promise:

> “Understand the classic quickly, then read the original with confidence.”

The revenue path is:

1. Free discovery portal.
2. Community reading features.
3. Paid enhanced editions with original commentary, short versions, background and modern relevance.
4. Bundles for students, reading groups and lifelong learners.

The original contribution matters commercially and legally. Public-domain text alone is freely available elsewhere; the paid value must be the framing, guide, curation and learning experience.

## 2. Recommended pricing

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Portal access | Free | Search, browse, save, comments/reviews |
| Kindle/eBook enhanced edition | £2.99 | Original guide + public-domain text; price sensitive |
| Direct EPUB/PDF | £1.99–£2.99 | Higher margin if sold directly |
| Paperback | £9.99–£12.99 | Print-on-demand; margin depends on page count |
| Hardback | £17.99–£24.99 | Gift/collector edition for selected titles |
| Reader club | £12/year | Reading plans, notes, early access, book-club guides |
| School/book-club pack | £49/year | Discussion guides, printable summaries, group pages |

### Pricing recommendation

Start with Kindle/eBook and paperback. Add hardbacks only for the top titles after demand is proven.

## 3. Competitor analysis

| Competitor / alternative | Current model | Strength | Weakness / opening for Lightning Classics |
|---|---|---|---|
| Project Gutenberg | Free public-domain ebooks | Huge free catalogue | Text-first; limited modern teaching, short versions and implications for today |
| Standard Ebooks | Free carefully formatted public-domain editions | High-quality ebooks | Not built around short summaries, learning pathway or commercial print bundles |
| Amazon public-domain editions | Many low-cost/free editions | Easy purchase and Kindle delivery | Quality varies; many editions lack original guide/context |
| SparkNotes / study guides | Study summaries and analysis | Familiar student market | Often separate from the full text; Lightning can bundle text + guide + short version |
| Audible/LibriVox | Audio access | Convenient listening | Less focused on “understand quickly then read” |
| Premium classics publishers | Beautiful editions | Gift value | Expensive; Lightning can be accessible and practical |

## 4. Go-to-market strategy

### Initial niches

1. Adults who want to read classics but feel intimidated.
2. Book clubs needing discussion structure.
3. Students needing fast context before reading.
4. Teachers/tutors/home educators needing low-cost material.
5. Gift buyers for accessible classics.

### First 90 days

1. Select first 20 high-demand classics.
2. Create a repeatable edition template: original text, background, lightning version, themes, today implications, discussion questions.
3. Publish 5 flagship editions first, not 50 weak ones.
4. Use portal pages as SEO landing pages for each title.
5. Launch reader club at £12/year.
6. Create book-club guides for each flagship title.
7. Sell Kindle first; add print when formatting stabilises.

### Sales message

> “Classic books with the context you wish you had: the original text, a lightning version, background, themes and why it still matters.”

## 5. Product improvements to maximise community size

### Portal features

- Reading paths: “Start here”, “Gothic classics”, “Short classics”, “Political classics”, “Books under 200 pages”.
- Difficulty and time-to-read labels.
- “Read the lightning version first” option.
- Book-club discussion prompts.
- User shelves and reading lists.
- Quotes and theme tags.
- “Implications for today” comments.

### Publishing workflow

- Template-driven title production.
- Rights/public-domain verification checklist by territory.
- Original commentary word-count requirement.
- KDP metadata templates.
- Cover templates.
- Quality-control checklist for typos, formatting and citations.

### Retention

- Monthly reading challenge.
- “Classic of the month”.
- Book-club pages.
- Completion badges.
- Email series: background before each chapter/week.

## 9. Commercial risks and mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Free public-domain alternatives undercut paid books | Sell guide/context/short version/today relevance, not just text |
| Public-domain rights vary by territory | Maintain rights checklist and territory notes |
| KDP royalty limitations for public-domain content | Add substantial original content and understand 35% vs 70% eligibility |
| Catalogue quality drops as scale grows | Use strict production templates and QA |
| Low search traffic | Build SEO pages and reading-path content |
